Transportation

FROM/TO GUARULHOS AIRPORT (GRU)
Guarulhos airport is 25 km from São Paulo. You can hit SP from GRU by car, train, or bus.
CAR: The only licensed taxi company at São Paulo Airport is , they work with fixed prices, and you can pay at the totems located at the exits on all terminals. You can also take Uber in one of the 4 Uber totems outside the arrivals floor: T1 (near Check-in A), T2 (near Check-in C and D), and T3 (near Check-in H). The price varies considerably, more or less in the range of R$ 100,00 - R$250,00.
TRAIN: There are two routes available to the downtown. Airport Express (only 3 stops up to Luz station) and Line 13 Jade (up to Eng Goulart station) + Line 12 Safira (up to Brás station), all operated by CPTM.
BUS: There is a bus line from GRU Airport to the center of São Paulo called Airport Bus Service . You can take it near the ground transportation area. The ticket costs approximately R$ 40,00.

FROM/TO CONGONHAS AIRPORT (CGH)
The easiest way of leaving or arriving at CGH is by car, but other options exist.
CAR: You can take a taxi at the arrivals hall (ground floor) or an Uber on the underground floor.
BUS: The nearest bus stop is the arrivals hall, where you can take the 609J-10 route, which departures every 15 minutes, from 05:20h to 23:40h. There is also a bus stop 650m from the arrivals hall, with several routes available, a trip from there up to Av. Paulista (routes 5175-10, 5178-10, or 5185-10) takes 45 min, to the downtowns (by route 175T-10) takes 40min. The bus schedules and routes can be found on Google Maps with very good accuracy.
METRO: The nearest metro station is São Judas, on the Blue line. It is located about 3km from the airport. To get there, take the bus outside the arrivals hall. You can use routes 609J-10 to São Judas metro station, which runs every 15 minutes between 5 am and 11:40 pm. The trip to the metro station takes 15-20 minutes. From São Judas station, it takes around 25 min to Av. Paulista (Blue line up to Paraíso, then Green line up to Trianon-Masp station) and 20 minutes to downtown São Paulo (Sé station).

TRANSPORTATION INSIDE SÃO PAULO CITY
You can take the subway (metro), bus, train, or taxi/Uber to move around the city.
METRO: You can hit many places in the city by metro, find the SP's metro map here. A single-trip ticket costs R$4,40.
BUS: The bus schedules and routes can be found on Google Maps with very good accuracy. A single trip costs R$4,40 and can be paid inside the bus in cash (use small denominations and/or coins).
TAXI / UBER: Uber and taxi rides are available all over the city via Apps. They usually accept payment by card as well as in cash. The rates can change depending on traffic in the city. You need to install and register previously, see https://www.uber.com/

FROM PINHEIROS NEIGHBORHOOD TO IME-USP
You can take the metro (Yellow line - 4) towards Butantã station. From there you can take one of the following buses: 701U-10, 702U-10, 7181-10, 7411-10, or 7412-10.
Two bus lines operate primarily inside the campus and go up to the Butantã metro station. The bus lines are 8012 and 8022; you can see their routes here .
